1. Lavandula stoechas (Spanish/French Lavender)
Mechanism of Action:

Terpenes and phenolics act as smooth muscle relaxants via calcium channel blockade.

Mild sedative/anxiolytic effects via central nervous system modulation.

Benefits:

Relief of gastrointestinal spasms.

Mild anxiety and nervous tension.

Respiratory relaxation (mild bronchodilation).

Precautions:

Avoid in patients with allergy to Lamiaceae family plants.

Use cautiously with sedatives.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL, 2–3 times daily.

Capsule/Extract: 50–100 mg standardized extract/day (traditional herbal dose).

Side Effects:

Rare: mild sedation, nausea, skin irritation.

2. Malva sylvestris (Common Mallow)
Mechanism of Action:

High mucilage content → forms protective coating on mucous membranes.

Antioxidant and mild laxative effects.

Benefits:

Soothes throat irritation, cough, and gastrointestinal mucosa.

Mild laxative for constipation.

Precautions:

Generally safe; avoid in rare allergies.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–8 mL/day.

Capsule/Extract: 200–400 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Rare: mild diarrhea or bloating.

3. Althaea officinalis (Marshmallow Root)
Mechanism of Action:

Mucilage → demulcent, anti-inflammatory.

Protects respiratory and digestive mucosa.

Benefits:

Cough, sore throat relief.

Anti-inflammatory for GI tract.

Precautions:

Avoid taking at same time as other oral medications (may reduce absorption).

Dose:

Syrup: 2–8 mL/day.

Capsule/Extract: 300–500 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Very rare: mild GI upset.

4. Glycyrrhiza glabra (Licorice Root)
Mechanism of Action:

Glycyrrhizin → anti-inflammatory, immunomodulatory, antioxidant.

Mimics cortisol → influences adrenal hormones.

Benefits:

Cough and throat irritation.

Anti-inflammatory in respiratory and digestive systems.

Precautions:

Avoid in hypertension, kidney disease, or long-term use (>4–6 weeks).

May interact with diuretics, steroids, or antihypertensives.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL, 2–3 times/day.

Capsule/Extract: 100–300 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Hypertension, fluid retention, low potassium, headache (rare at high doses).

5. Ziziphus jujube (Jujube)
Mechanism of Action:

Flavonoids → antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, mild sedative.

Modulates stress response and immunity.

Benefits:

Reduces stress and anxiety.

Supports sleep.

Mild immune support.

Precautions:

Avoid in severe diabetes (may affect blood sugar).

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL/day.

Capsule/Extract: 200–500 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Rare: drowsiness, mild GI upset.

6. Cassia fistula (Golden Shower Tree)
Mechanism of Action:

Anthraquinones → laxative, antioxidant, anti-inflammatory.

Tannins → antimicrobial.

Benefits:

Relief of constipation.

Anti-inflammatory for GI tract.

Precautions:

Avoid chronic use (risk of diarrhea, electrolyte imbalance).

Not recommended in pregnancy.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL/day (short-term).

Capsule/Extract: 100–300 mg/day (standardized).

Side Effects:

Diarrhea, abdominal cramp, electrolyte imbalance with long-term use.

7. Viola odorata (Sweet Violet)
Mechanism of Action:

Phenolic compounds → anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, expectorant.

Benefits:

Soothes cough and respiratory irritation.

Mild sedative effect.

Precautions:

Rare allergy possible.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL/day.

Capsule/Extract: 100–300 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Rare: mild GI upset, headache.

8. Terminalia chebula (Haritaki)
Mechanism of Action:

Tannins and ellagic acid → antioxidant, antimicrobial, digestive tonic.

Modulates bowel motility and gut microbiota.

Benefits:

Improves digestion, mild laxative.

Supports immune function.

Antioxidant and metabolic health.

Precautions:

Avoid if severe diarrhea.

Use cautiously in pregnancy.

Dose:

Syrup: 2–5 mL/day.

Capsule/Extract: 300–500 mg/day.

Side Effects:

Diarrhea (high doses), abdominal discomfort.
